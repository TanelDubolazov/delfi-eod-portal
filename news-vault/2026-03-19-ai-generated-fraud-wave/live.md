Fraudsters are using AI to mimic relatives' voices to request money under the guise of an emergency. Victims have already lost hundreds of thousands of euros.

### Hyper-Realistic Deepfake Scams

A sophisticated new wave of AI-driven fraud is targeting Estonian bank customers, leaving local law enforcement and cybersecurity experts scrambling. The **Police and Border Guard Board (PPA)** has issued an urgent warning regarding a highly convincing form of 'emergency' scam that utilizes hyper-realistic **Deepfake voice** technology. Unlike traditional phone scams that rely on a generic, stressed voice, these attackers are harvesting short voice samples of victims' children or relatives from public social media profiles.

Using readily available, powerful AI cloning algorithms, the scammers generate a high-fidelity synthetic voice that can repeat any script with natural intonation, breathing patterns, and even localized accents. The typical scenario involves a "distress call" in the voice of a panicked relative claiming they have been in a serious car accident, detained by foreign police, or are facing an acute medical emergency. The synthetic voice requests an immediate, large-sum transfer—often **€5,000 to €15,000**—to a provided IBAN number to resolve the crisis.

---

### Critical Losses and Analysis

"The emotional leverage is total," says Chief Inspector **Jelizaveta Kaledina** of the PPA's Cybercrime Unit. "When you hear your own daughter's voice crying and pleading for help, you don't stop to analyze the technical details; you panic and pay." The synthetic calls often include simulated background noise, such as sirens or static, to further enhance the emergency narrative and mask any minor digital artifacts in the generated audio.

According to preliminary data, the average financial loss per successful victim in March has skyrocketed, with total damages already exceeding **€650,000**. The PPA's digital forensics team is currently analyzing hundreds of reported call recordings, looking for "acoustic fingerprints" that might link the different attacks to a specific, high-end AI model or coordinated crime group.

### Official PPA Warnings

Residents are being advised to implement immediate, mandatory verification protocols for any distress call involving financial requests. The PPA suggests setting a unique "crisis codeword" known only to immediate family members. If you receive such a call, remain calm, hang up immediately, and try to contact the relative on their known, legitimate number, even if the incoming call appeared to be from that number due to caller ID spoofing.

Banks are also implementing advanced AI detection algorithms on high-value transfers that match known fraud patterns. The current status of this fraud wave is marked as "Critical," with the PPA expecting attacks to increase in sophistication as AI technology continues to evolve.
