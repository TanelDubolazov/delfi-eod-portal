The number of infections has tripled in a week. Hospitals have implemented visitation bans, and the shortage of beds has become critical.

### Rapid Spread of "Subclade K" Variant

The Estonian Health Board has issued an urgent nationwide alert regarding a highly mutated strain of Influenza A (H3N2), identified as **Subclade K**. This variant is spreading significantly faster than typical seasonal flu, catching many local clinics off guard. While not officially classified as a "super-flu," its seven distinct mutations allow it to bypass some existing community immunity.

Unlike previous years, this wave is hitting children and young adults with unusual intensity. In the last 48 hours, emergency rooms in **Tallinn** and **Tartu** have reported a massive surge in patients under the age of 18. Doctors are seeing a consistent pattern of "spike fevers" reaching **40°C** or higher within hours of the first symptom. The rapid onset of severe fatigue has left thousands unable to attend work or school.

---

### Healthcare System Under Pressure

The pressure on the healthcare system is reaching a breaking point as staff absences mirror the infection rate. The Family Doctor Advisory Line (1220) is currently handling over **1,200 calls per day**, a record for the month of March. Health officials are urging citizens to stay home at the first sign of a scratchy throat. "We are seeing a vertical climb in cases that mimics the early days of the 2020 pandemic," says Chief Specialist **Dr. Mari Maasikas**.

### Mandatory Restrictions and Safety

In response to the data, the Ministry of Education has advised all schools in the **Harju** and **Tartu** regions to switch to distance learning for at least ten days. Public gatherings are being discouraged, and several large-scale sporting events have been postponed. The Health Board stresses that while the current vaccine is not a perfect match for Subclade K, it still significantly reduces the risk of severe illness.

Residents are advised to maintain strict hand hygiene and wear masks in crowded public transport. If you or your child develop a high fever that does not respond to paracetamol, contact your family doctor immediately via digital channels rather than visiting the ER in person. The current red alert status for respiratory viruses will remain in place until the infection curve shows clear signs of plateauing.
